On the demise of their High Priest Sardar Dastur Nosherwan Jamasp on 27th October 1884, the Parsee Community raised subscriptions to perpetuate the memory of their deeply venerated spiritual head, and the Sardar Dastur Nosherwan Girls' High School came into being.

This School first started in 1893 in House #317, Main Street (then Wyse's Bakery and now Central Bank of India). The three surviving brothers of the Dastur family presented this house to the school in memory of their sister the Late Bai Behmanbai. Later on the school shifted from 317, Main Street to Bungalow No. 3, Arsenal Road, now Moledina Road. It was inaugurated in 1893 by Khan Bahadur Meher Hoshang Dastur. The Sardar Dastur Nosherwan Girls' High School's Founder's Day is celebrated on 1st February every year.

In 1911 to accommodate a larger number of children, a new building was erected in memory of Bai Behmanbai Pestonji Kanga by her brothers. The building was designed by Mr. Burjor S. Jamshed Aga of the well known firm of Architects M/s. Shapurji N. Chandabhoy and Co. Bombay. Between 1920-30, Lady Dhunjbhoy Bomanji and Minocher Girls' Hostel buildings were added.

"The old has always to make for the new and that is the law of life."

The Beautiful quaint school building that stood like a fortress in all its magnificence providing quality education to young girls over generations probably was to adore itself in a robe befitting changing times... 2017,the year,to mark the Quasquicentennial Celebration saw a magnanimous state-of-the-art new building with promising new infrastructure assuring girls across all sections with high standard of education .

In its 130 years of existence , Sardar Nosherwan Girls'High School has built a reputation of being one of the leading educational institutions for girls.

Our Vision

We envision sending out into the world mature young ladies who have been exposed to various learning experiences at school and are ready to excel in the field of their choice. They are eager to contribute to society while at the same time selectively picking up the good from there. Our young ladies are open to developing a symbiotic relationship with the world at large. We see our girls as leaders of tomorrow with futuristic outlook.

Our Mission

Our Mission is to help the child realize her own potential.We facilitate the child to draw from within while at the same time exposing her steadily to various learning experiences. We encourage our students to compete with themselves and set targets for bettering themselves each time. The integrated guidance and development programme at school thus provided helps us to achieve our mission and their dreams.

Ideas

To educate actually means to facilitate the child to draw from within. Each child is an entity and has different talents, interests and inclinations. Keeping this in mind, at Dastur Girls’ we allow each child to express herself in the medium of her choice – Art , Drama, Elocution, Writing, Poetry, Sports , Games, Music, Culinary skills etc. We send into the world a mature young lady having positive civilisational values and who is ' future ready' to accept challenges, with a fully rounded personality.

Our Houses

To inculcate competitive spirit and espirit de corps, the girls are grouped into 4 houses – Tata (Green House), Bhabha (Violet House), Dastur (Yellow House) and Wadia (Red House).

Our Team Members

Our teaching staff comprises a team of highly qualified, dedicated, and compassionate educators who strive to bring out the best in every child. Each teacher blends professional expertise with personal care, nurturing young minds to become confident, creative, and responsible individuals.

Our school is a recognised Secondary School and admits girls without distinction of caste and creed.
